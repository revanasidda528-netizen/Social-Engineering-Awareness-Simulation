1\. Introduction:

Social engineering is a manipulation technique used by cyber attackers to trick people into revealing confidential information such as passwords, OTPs, banking information, or personal details. Unlike traditional hacking, social engineering targets human psychology instead of technical vulnerabilities.

One of the most common forms of social engineering is phishing. Phishing attacks are usually performed through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear genuine and trustworthy. Attackers use urgency, fear, or attractive offers to manipulate users into clicking malicious links or sharing sensitive information.

This project focuses on creating a safe phishing email simulation for educational and awareness purposes. The simulation helps users understand how phishing emails look and how they can identify suspicious activities online.

2\. Objective:

3\. Problem Statement:

Many internet users become victims of phishing attacks because they are unaware of common warning signs found in fake emails and fraudulent messages. Lack of cybersecurity awareness increases the risk of data theft, identity theft, and financial fraud.

This project aims to educate users about phishing threats through a simulated awareness campaign.
